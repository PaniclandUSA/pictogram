## DESIGN PRINCIPLES

### Monochrome Conversion Rules
**From color semantics to geometric semantics:**

1. **Stroke Weight Hierarchy**
   - Primary elements: 12-16px (core meaning)
   - Secondary elements: 8-10px (context)
   - Detail elements: 4-6px (refinement)

2. **Semantic Encoding**
   - **Harm/Damage/Conflict** (was red) → Dashed strokes (8,6 or 12,8 patterns)
   - **Growth/Healing/Restoration** (was green) → Solid strokes
   - **Core/Essential/Security** (was solid fill) → Filled circles/shapes
   - **External/Boundary** (was heavy outline) → Thicker stroke weight

3. **Pattern Library**
   - Crack texture: Irregular dashed lines for damage
   - Stitch marks: Regular dashed lines for repair
   - Radiating lines: Growth/emergence
   - Concentric rings: Containment/boundaries

4. **Scalability**
   - All glyphs designed for 16px font size minimum
   - Pure black (#000000) only
   - White used for cutouts/negative space only
   - No gradients, no opacity variations in final forms

### Temporal Mechanics Integration
ChronoCore glyphs (24 total) provide temporal scaffolding:
- **Time measurement** (E00A-E00F): Duration, rhythm, discrete steps
- **Causality** (E01A-E01F): Cause-effect, ordering, sequence
- **Temporal boundaries** (E03A-E03F): Deadlines, windows, expiry
- **State transitions** (E04A-E04F): Past/present/future, snapshots, loops

Without temporal mechanics, all other glyphs exist in "eternal now." ChronoCore makes the entire system capable of planning, remembering, promising, regretting, and enduring through time.
